Thomas McQuillan is a photographer from Wellington, New Zealand. His work is situated in the realm of documentary photography, with a particular focus and affection for the unseen or unloved scenes and phenomena of the urban and suburban environment. His photographs negotiate a precipitous space between a poetic reverence for light and surface of the banal, contrasted with a critical eye for the untethered corporate creep of modern capitalism.

His project, The Sum of Things (2012), is the culmination of his Master of Fine Arts degree program at Massey University. It is an exploration and critique of the insidious influence and reach of consumerist culture, leveraging the still photograph's unique ability to arrest time and enable critical reflection. However, photographs are entrenched in the consumer realm, illustrating commodities and perpetuating consumerist culture. The thesis unpacks the ramifications of this convoluted relationship, examining various strategies for overcoming the baggage of the spectacle, including: detournement, ambiguity, banality, and the poetic.
