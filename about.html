Thomas McQuillan is a Photographer based in Bor√•s, Sweden. His work spans the genres of Landscape, Still-Life and Street Photography, in an exploration of the ordinary and often disregarded spaces of the urban and suburban landscape. In his work, he attempts to negotiate a precipitous junction between his affection for the poetry of form, surface and light, with his distrust of corporate consumer culture and its untethered creep into modern life.

McQuillan's project, The Sum of Things (2012), is the culmination of his MFA Degree while at Massey University. In a critical survery of consumer culture, McQuillan leverages still photography's unmatched talent for plucking disparate scenes and elements out of the passage of time, raising a mirror to the oddness and dysfunction of our time.

However, photographs are entrenched in the consumer realm - illustrating commodities and perpetuating consumerist culture. His thesis attempts to unpack the ramifications of this convoluted relationship, exploring strategies for overcoming the baggage of the spectacle, including detournement, ambiguity, banality, and the poetic.
